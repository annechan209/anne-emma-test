More details

Michelle lives with her family in a house owned by the council, but a few years ago they had a very different way of life. They lived in a caravan and travelled around a lot, mostly in Cheshire, where she went to school. Then her dad got seriously ill and they had to settle on a council-owned site. Eventually, someone from the council helped them to get on the waiting list, and they eventually got somewhere, in part due to her father’s ill health.

Michelle used to work as a childminder, looking after children from some of the families in her community. But when her dad’s condition got worse, she decided to start caring for him full time. After he had an assessment, he started claiming PIP in 2015.

Devices and technology

Michelle and her family don’t own a computer. They have an old smartphone that the whole house – and even some of their neighbours – share. But it tends to stay in a drawer until someone needs it. It doesn’t often have enough credit to make a call.

If Michelle needs to go on the internet, she’ll use one of the computers at her local library. She uses email and social media to keep in touch with people, but she’s not particularly active online.

Goals and wishes

Michelle would like to claim some money to help her with the cost of looking after her dad. Someone she grew up with has told her about something called Carer’s Allowance and offered to help her apply. She’s got no idea what she’ll need to do or how much she might get. But she’s booked one of the library computers to try to find out a bit more information online.

Frustrations

Michelle is frustrated with the way everything’s going online. She’s completely comfortable using a computer, but it can be harder for her to do certain things on the internet than face to face.

She’s never left the country so she doesn’t have a passport and she’s only ever been paid cash in hand. That can make things harder if she needs to do something “official” and there’s no record of her.

In contrast, she’s used a helpline to get information about her dad’s PIP money and gone into the council office to speak to them about their housing benefit. They just asked her some questions about her family to make sure they were dealing with the right person. That seemed a lot easier.

Making things better for Michelle
